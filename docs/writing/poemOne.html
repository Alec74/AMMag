Poem

The Sparkle Between

By Alec McGlone

Home

Till it be, the new morrow.

Till it be, my new flower.

Till it be, the shine of a star.

Should I be, it was made narrow.

Should I be, they will cower.

Should I be, a desire was engulfed.

Be it me, I found the island.

Be it me, she shone through.

Be it me, her stone to a star.

Till they see, one’s might to force.

Till they see, one’s might to spar.

Till they see, their shared fight.

Till they see, their only desire.

Till they see, one only fate.

Till they see, one only shine.

Till they see, the light to an eye.

Till they see, that one is only fire.

Till they see, that one silhouette go.

Till they see, that one memory remain.

Till they see, an old remnant decay away.

Till they see, one’s love to another entity.
