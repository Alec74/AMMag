Flash Fiction

Mother's Room

By Alec McGlone

Home

That room, every step I question if I’m still in that room. She tugs on my hand. My feet falter. I’m back. The screams drown out the world and I drift like a cloud in a grey sky. My tongue tries to form a word but my mouth doesn’t open. I can’t remember what it felt like to talk. All I remember are the screams of that room. He put me there. I can’t recall who but I know we were close. He trapped me in that room. Time skipped until nothing was real. I’m lost now.

I step forward slowly. The girl leading me by hand keeps pulling me, faster and faster. My feet barely keep up. I see a building in the distance. It looks dark compared to the bright sky above and the heated sand we stomp through. She must be aiming us towards it. We’re almost there.

“Say something. Anything! I know you’re still in there!”

The words barely register. That room. Did they come from the room? I’m too terrified to run. I stop in my tracks. The girl turns around to look at me. I cover my ears with my hands to drown out the screams. I’m back in the room. Hands reach out from the walls trying to grasp something. Voices start yelling, my ears still hear them, there's no escape. I look towards the building, while my eyes drip with tears. I’m trapped with no escape. Who is this girl?

“We have to keep moving. He’ll find us any moment now.”

The tugging on my hand resumes. I run again, eyes dripping all the way. We find the door to the building ajar. The girl leads me inside. We hear a noise from a room nearby. The handle twists and the door opens. A blonde haired man walks out and looks like he was expecting visitors.

“You made it. Has she spoken at all since you freed her,” he asks.

“Daniel, who sent you?” the girl asks.

“I sent myself. There’s only so much Xander can control,” Daniel said.

They pause for a moment.

“No, she's been mute ever since I freed her from Xander’s simulation,” the girl said.

“I can’t imagine what it must have been like. What did Xander do to her in there? What was done that made me?” Daniel exclaims.

“The all knowing robot. Only worth the life of one wife,” the girl said, shaking her head.

“Come let me show you what this robot has done for you,” Daniel said.

He turns to start walking. The girl still holding my hand pulls me after him. We enter a room holding a large metallic sphere jutting out from a larger machine. Wires spread all across the two and along the floor. The larger ones lead to a computer screen.

“I stole my work from Xander’s research team and brought it here to the desert. With my atomic collider you can build perfectly habitable star systems in an instant! An entire galaxy designed by hand!” Daniel said excitedly.

I stare at the screen. I lose myself in its light. The answer lies in its power. I want to say something.

“What about the war?” the girl asks.

“There won’t be a war left to fight with infinite natural resources. We’ll pay to end it! Xander created me to build what he could only dream of. We have to use it quickly before that mad man tracks down its energy signature,” Daniel said.

My tongue twitches in my mouth. I slip my hand from the girl’s grasp. I open my mouth.

“Aunghow,” I blurt out.

“Mom!” The girl said.

She stares at me expecting more. Her brown eyes piercing my heart. She called me Mom?

“N– Now,” I said.

It took all my effort to muster that one word. I see the room. I know how to escape it. I wave my arms in the air. They both turn to face me. I focus harder than I ever have before.

“Let… Me… Use…” I squeak the words out.

“You want to use the terminal? It's a highly dangerous machine but incredibly interpretive. I’ve saved the blueprint of our galaxy and was designing its improvement,” Daniel said.

The room closes in around me, assaulting my senses. I close my eyes and put my hands over my ears. The room is bright, the hands tug at my body. I let out a yell, shaking my companions. She called me mom.

“Dau– Daughter?” I said.

I open my eyes and stare. I see fear in her eyes.

“Yes mom?” The girl says.

“What is your name?” I ask.

The girl looks at the floor. She whispers, “Clare.”

I nod and walk to the terminal. I find the data for our active galaxy and type out my wishes. The words light up green telling me it's ready to be executed.

I look behind me. Clare has a far away look in her eye. Daniel is staring at the screen. He gasps.

“All my work… For nothing. I should stop you,” He said.

“We’re all trapped in Xander’s game. This is the only way to protect more people from his plague,” I said.

Clare is staring at the machine, oblivious to what is going on. I turn back to the terminal.

“Husband,” I said.

“Xander.” Clare says softly.

I nod and hit enter.

I blink once. Fractured memories flood back. I want to tell the world. I remember Chloe is my name. Then the world falls away.
